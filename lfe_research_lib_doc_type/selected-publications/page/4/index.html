Experiential Learning and Its Critics: Preserving the Role of Experience in Management Learning and Education

Kayes, D. C. 2002, Academy of Management Learning and Education,1(2): 137-149. Pdf, 38 Pages.

This paper considers John Dewey’s dual reformist-preservationist agenda for education in the context of current debates about the role of experience in management learning. The paper argues for preserving experience-based approaches to management learning by revising the concept of experience to more clearly account for the relationship between personal and social (i.e. , tacit/explicit) knowledge.
